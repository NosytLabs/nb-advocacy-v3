## 3. 📊 UP-TO-DATE DATA POINTS (Verified from Sources)

### Child Poverty (Feb 2026 Report Card - Campaign 2000 / Human Development Council)
- **21.9% child poverty rate** in NB in 2023 (most recent data, unchanged from 2022)
- **32,140 children** living in poverty
- **41.2% child poverty rate** in Campbellton (highest in province)
- **30.7%** in Saint John (Ward 3: 44.8%)
- **51.2%** on Indigenous reserves
- **47.8%** in single-parent households vs 11.3% in two-parent
- NB has **4th highest** child poverty rate among provinces
- Top 10% of families hold **22.9%** of total income; bottom 10% hold **1.6%**
- Sources: CBC Feb 25 2026 (https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/new-brunswick/child-poverty-alarmingly-high-in-n-b-says-human-development-council-9.7105164), Campaign 2000 PDF (https://campaign2000.ca/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/2025-NB-Report-Card-on-Child-and-Family-Poverty.pdf)

### Healthcare Wait Times & ER Crisis (Sep 2025 MEI Report)
- **41,000+ patients** left Horizon ERs without treatment in 2024-25
- **12.9% walkout rate** (national average: 7.8%) - 3rd highest in Canada
- **22,000 Vitalité patients** also walked out (11.5% rate)
- Combined: **63,000+ NB patients** left ERs untreated
- **635 ALC patients** occupying hospital beds (target: 330)
- **40%+ of inpatient beds** occupied by ALC (alternate level of care) patients
- **238,000 NBers** without a family doctor or nurse practitioner
- **121 patients died** while on surgical/diagnostic waitlists (2024-25)
- **$173.9M** travel nurse scandal (CHL paid $300.72/hour per nurse)
- Walkout trend: 9.5% (2021) → 12% (2022) → 12% (2023) → 12.9% (2024-25)
- Sources: CBC Sep 19 2025 (https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/new-brunswick/new-brunswick-emergency-patient-leave-horizon-mei-report-vitalit%C3%A9-1.7637201), MEI Report (https://www.iedm.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/2025-09-18-NR-Leaving-ER-NB.pdf)

### NB Power Debt & Energy
- **$5.9 Billion** total debt (carried by NB Power)
- Point Lepreau nuclear operated **only 27%** of time in 2024-25
- **$9 million/day** in replacement power costs during peak demand
- **$1 Billion RIGS gas plant** deal with Missouri-based PROENERGY
- Potential **$3.5 Billion** cost over 25 years
- **$45 million "tariff relief"** grant to Irving Paper (early 2026)
- **19.4% cumulative rate hike** for residential users
- **1 in 4 households** suffer from energy poverty (>6% income on heating)
- Sources: Existing page content, CBC NB coverage

### Housing & Homelessness (2025 Data)
- **830 unhoused** in Moncton
- **437 unhoused** in Saint John
- **275 unhoused** in Fredericton
- **1,500+ chronically homeless** across major cities
- **440 youth** (ages 16-26) homeless in 2025
- **100+ unhoused deaths** in 2025 (carfentanil/toxic supply)
- 50-100% rent increases, mass "renovictions"
- Sources: Existing page content, HDC data

### Irving/Oligarchy Data
- Irving Oil refinery assessed at **$107M** (increased only $10.2M in 10 years)
- US refineries pay **5x more** property taxes than Irving Oil
- **42-year** Canaport crude-oil tank farm property tax exemption
- Refinery tax bill **fell by $25,165** in 2024 while residents face 50% hikes
- **$700,000 retroactive property tax refund** to J.D. Irving and Twin Rivers Paper (2023)
- **$30 Billion** estimated combined private wealth of Irving/McCain/Cooke families
- Point Lepreau property tax bill: **$7.3M/year** (1% of electricity output)
- Irving Oil output: **$10+ billion/year**, property tax bill **$1M less** than Lepreau
- Source: CBC investigative report (https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/new-brunswick/propery-taxes-irving-refineries-1.6671058)

### Corruption & Procurement
- **Tim Richardson**: Embezzled $700,000+ from Lt. Governor's office
- **Yassin Choukri**: Stole $481,000+ from law society trust (Deputy AG)
- **Daniel Bard / 3+ Corporation**: 19 RCMP charges, $1M+ fraud
